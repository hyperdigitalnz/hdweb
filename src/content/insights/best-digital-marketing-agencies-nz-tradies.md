## 2. TradesUp / Digital Tradie

**Based in:** Auckland
**Best for:** Tradies wanting a packaged website-plus-SEO solution
**Services:** Websites, SEO, Google Ads, social media

TradesUp (previously Digital Tradie) is one of the more established names in the NZ
tradie-marketing space. They offer templated websites paired with SEO and Google Ads
management. Their main pitch is an all-in-one monthly package aimed at trades.

**Strengths:** They understand the trades market and have been around long enough to build a
reputation. Their website templates are trade-specific and their onboarding is
straightforward.

**Watch out for:** Template-based websites can limit your ability to stand out, especially
if several competitors in your area are using the same provider. Ask to see the actual ad
accounts rather than summary reports, so you can verify the numbers.

## 4. Trojan Digital / TopRankd

**Based in:** Various NZ locations
**Best for:** Tradies looking for SEO-focused growth
**Services:** SEO, Google Ads, web design, content marketing

Several NZ agencies (like Trojan Digital and TopRankd) have carved out a niche working
with trade and service businesses. They tend to lean heavier on SEO and content marketing
as the primary growth channel.

**Strengths:** If you're playing the long game and want to invest in organic search
rankings, SEO-first agencies can build a strong foundation. Good content strategies attract
customers who are researching before they buy.

**Watch out for:** SEO takes months to show results. If you need the phone ringing next
week, SEO alone won't do it (we wrote a [whole article on the ads-vs-SEO question](/insights/google-ads-vs-seo-for-tradies)). Make sure the agency is transparent
about timelines and doesn't promise page-one rankings by a specific date.

## 5. General digital agencies with trade clients

Agencies like Firefly Digital, Sprocket Digital, and Adhesion are full-service shops that
work across many industries, including trades. They bring broad digital marketing
experience and often have larger creative teams.

**Strengths:** Wider range of services (branding, video, PR). Good if your business is
growing beyond pure lead generation and you need more sophisticated marketing.

**Watch out for:** Trades are one of many verticals they serve. Your campaign manager may
not know the difference between a hot water cylinder install and a full re-pipe, which
affects keyword strategy and ad copy. Ask how many trade clients they manage and whether
they have trade-specific case studies with verifiable numbers.

## How to choose the right agency

Regardless of who you pick, here's what to ask before signing anything:

1. **"Can I see the actual ad account?"** Any agency worth hiring will give you full access
   to your own Google Ads and Analytics accounts. If they build campaigns in their own
   account and just send you screenshots, you'll lose everything if you leave.

2. **"What's my cost per lead, and how do you track it?"** Clicks and impressions don't pay
   the bills. You need to know how many actual enquiries your ads are generating and what
   each one costs.

3. **"Do you have case studies in my trade?"** A plumber's campaign looks nothing like a
   builder's. Ask for results from businesses like yours, not just a logo wall.

4. **"What happens if I leave?"** You should own your website, your domain, your ad
   accounts and your data. If the agency keeps any of it, that's a red flag.

5. **"How long is the contract?"** Month-to-month is a sign the agency trusts their own
   results. Long lock-in contracts protect the agency, not you.
